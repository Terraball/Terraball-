About TerraBall

Small natural gifts with the power to restore green spaces.

TerraBall are made with seeds, natural soil, compost, and clay. They protect native and suitable seeds until rain and soil conditions help them germinate.

They can be thrown on open soil, garden areas, farms, school grounds, or plantation zones. During rains, each TerraBall slowly breaks down and the seed can grow into a plant.

Our mission is to encourage sustainable gifting and help people contribute to restoring nature.

FAQ

Common questions.

What are TerraBall?

TerraBall are small seed balls made with seeds, natural soil, compost, and clay. The outer layer protects the seed, keeps it together, and makes planting simple. They are useful for gifting because every guest receives something meaningful that can grow into a plant.

How do they grow?

Place or gently throw the TerraBall on open soil, a farm edge, garden area, school ground, or plantation zone. When it receives rain or regular watering, the clay and compost layer softens, the seed gets moisture, and germination can begin when the season and soil are suitable.

How long before they germinate?

Germination time depends on the seed type, soil condition, water, sunlight, and season. Some seeds may begin in 7 to 21 days after good moisture, while tree seeds may take longer. Best results usually come during the rainy season or with regular light watering.
